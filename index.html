Intro

I am the Mutual of Omaha Distinguished Chair of Information Science & Technology and Professor of Information Systems and Quantitative Analysis in the College of Information Science & Technology at the University of Nebraska Omaha. Prior to the University of Nebraska Omaha, I was a faculty member at the University of Wisconsin - Eau Claire and Case Western Reserve University

I grew up in Neenah Wisconsin and got my undergraduate degree in Biology at Iowa State University, my Masters in Information Systems at Colorado State University, and my PhD in Information Systems at the University of Colorado at Boulder. Somewhere between Iowa State and Colorado State I even started a PhD in Entomology at Texas A&M University.

I have two kids, Maggie and Nate who currently live in Atlanta and Austin respectively. My wife, Liz, and I have been together for a long long time!

Research

Germonprez CV

I use qualitative, engaged research to investigate organizational participation with open communities and the dynamics of work found in these engagements. My research has been funded by numerous organizations including the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, the Ford Foundation, the National Science Foundation, the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, Mozilla, and the Wellcome Trust. I am the co-founder of the Linux Foundation Community Health Analytics OSS Project (CHAOSS). I have had work accepted at ISR, MISQ, JAIS, JIT, ISJ, I&O, CSCW, OpenSym, Group, HICSS, IEEE Computer, and ACM Interactions. I am also an active open source community member, having presented design and development work at LinuxCon, the Open Source Summit NA, the Linux Foundation Open Compliance Summit, the Linux Foundation Collaboration Summit, and the Open Source Member Summit.

Leisure

I love taking my Border Collie (Mister Stormy) for daily early morning runs to clear my mind (no earbuds - just owls, coyotes, and my footsteps). I am an avid wildflower gardener, and I love baking sweets - cookies and cakes. I've been known to fix up bikes and arcade games too.
